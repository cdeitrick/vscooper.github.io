Steffen Poltak received his PhD in Microbiology from UNH in May 2010, and is now an assistant professor at St. Anselm College in Manchester, NH.

Research Interests

Ecology and evolution of Burkholderia and Pseudomonas aeruginosa biofilm communities

Major research questions:

What happens in bacterial populations after prolonged selection in a biofilm environment?

What beneficial mutations are acquired under biofilm conditions?

How does biofilm diversity effect population productivity?

How does biofilm diversity arise and how do new members interact? Antagonistic / Synergistic/ or Neutral

Do evolved biofilm populations harbor genotypes that have increased virulence and/or increased antibiotic resistance?

How do biofilm subpopulations interact to survive? What are there contributions to the form and function of the biofilm community? Are the interactions synergistic, antagonistic, or something in between?

My interests lie not only in the interactions seen between various genotypes seen within a species, but also between those seen between different species.
