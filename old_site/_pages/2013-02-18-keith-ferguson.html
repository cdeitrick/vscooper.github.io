My graduate research in Dr. Cooper’s lab focused on how populations of Pseudomonas aeruginosa PA14 adapted to biofilm formation, development and dispersal. Broad goals of the project included measuring phenotypic and genetic diversity and quantifying fine-scale fitness effects of mutations observed in our model. A more specific goal included characterizing an adaptive allele in a polyphosphate kinase (ppk) gene.

As a recent graduate, I’m looking to find a career in the biotech field and am interested in the uses of genetically engineered microbes for industrial applications.
