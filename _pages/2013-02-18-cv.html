Research goals

  • To understand how bacteria evolutionarily adapt to new environments, such as in colonizing and causing disease in novel hosts. Biofilm-associated infection is our primary focus. This work is empowered by high-throughput genomic analysis of experimentally evolved strains and longitudinal studies of infectious isolates.
  • To advance fundamental understanding of evolutionary dynamics in structured communities, relevant to biofilms, solid tumors, and transitions to multicellularity
  • To use the gradient in genome evolutionary rates associated with replication timing and the cell cycle to improve genome legibility, understand speciation, and to guide more rational treatment of disease states
  • Propagate experimental evolution as a both a) a nuanced screen for gain-of-function mutations and b) a compelling method for teaching evolution-in-action
