I am an Undergraduate researcher that will be graduating with a B.S. in Microbiology in May 2013. I began working for Dr. Vaughn Cooper during my freshman year. Working in the Cooper lab provided me with an unique opportunity to experience a wide array of different fields including microbiology, genetics and ecology. I have worked on a variety of biofilm-related projects within both the Pseudomonas and Burkholderia systems.  I also preformed screens of environmental samples of Vibrio sp. isolated from the Great Bay for virulence factors. In the summer of 2011 I worked for Dr. Bill McDowell tracing nitrogen contamination by detecting mtDNA in water samples from the Great Bay watershed.

My interests include microbial ecology, social community interactions and connecting observed patterns to molecular mechanisms. I also enjoy hands-on work with lab equipment and coming up with creative solutions to problems encountered while researching. As a result, I am the go-to person for fixing lab equipment and have developed a passion for engineering. My long-term career goal is to work in a multidisciplinary field that allows me to combine all my passions in research and development of new technology as these fields advance.

I am passionate about teaching and I am always excited to collaborate with others. I can be contacted at mec99@wildcats.unh.edu
