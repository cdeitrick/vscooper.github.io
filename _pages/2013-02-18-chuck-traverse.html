I’m currently a technician in Dr. Vaughn Cooper’s lab studying adaptive history of a long term experimental evolution of B. cenocepacia. I received my Master's working in Vaughn's lab in the summer of 2012.

Interests and research questions:

What are the evolutionary steps toward building a stable ecological community from a founding clone?

When adapting to a specific environment, are there many ways to improve or will replicate communities follow a parallel evolutionary trajectory?

Using metagenomics of evolving populations to track beneficial alleles over time.

A major interest and ongoing effort of mine is figuring out why certain physiological changes that occur during an evolution result in a selective advantage. Does epistasis occur in genotypes with multiple mutations or does each mutation act alone?

What are the evolutionary dynamics between any two long-lasting genetic variants an a stable ecological community?
Clonal interference?
Cooperation?
Niche displacement?

How do bottlenecks and different selective regimes influence the origin and maintenance of genetic diversity?

How much genetic hitchhiking occurs in the progress of a selective sweep? Does a sweep of any fixed allele rely on subsequent selectively advantageous variation in order to take over a population or does variation exist on a fixed background as a result of the fixed allele?

Do any two isolates with similar phenotypes and/or genetic mutations behave in a similar manner in the community they arose in? Are they in competition or do they occupy separate niches?
